# Style Guide, Glossary & Recurring Objects

> Internal reference. The vocabulary substitutions below are load-bearing — they're the "invisible branding" that makes each institution's voice recognizable without a byline. Apply them consistently. Never explain them in-app.

## Per-institution vocabulary (never say / always say)

| Institution | Never says | Always says |
|---|---|---|
| Department of Extremely Important Research | discovered, found | concluded, observed, findings indicate |
| Committee for Poor Decisions | mistake, bad idea | approved outcome, decision of record |
| Museum of Useless History | old, ancient | historically underappreciated |
| Official Public Notices | don't, stop | residents are reminded |
| Neighborhood Wildlife Reports | attacked, aggressive, guess | engaged directly, displayed confidence, assessed with reasonable certainty |
| Lost & Found Authority | lost, unclaimed, missing | awaiting recognition, patiently held, separated from context |
| Bureau of Mild Emergencies | emergency, crisis, resolved | situation of moderate note, de-escalated to baseline |
| Office of Public Clarifications | (anything clarifying) | further clarification is not available at this time |

## Shared terminology (used across institutions)

- **Situation of moderate note** — the Bureau's standard classification for anything alarming enough to log, not alarming enough to escalate.
- **Approved outcome** — Committee-speak for any decision, regardless of result.
- **Awaiting recognition** — an item's official status at the Lost & Found Authority; never "unclaimed."
- **Historically underappreciated** — Museum-speak for old.
- **Context pending** — a Museum placard's default state when no one is sure what an exhibit is.
- **Duly noted and filed** — the closest any institution comes to dismissing something; always sincere.
- **Under continued non-clarification** — Office of Public Clarifications' term for any matter it has declined, again, to clarify.
- **Per the Tuesday Compromise** — invoked whenever consensus fails and a deadline is needed; see `TIMELINE.md`.

## Legendary objects

Recurring physical artifacts that can be referenced across institutions without re-explaining them. Treat these as established world furniture.

- **The Jointly-Held Filing Cabinet** — contains "historically significant nonsense," claimed by both the Museum and the Department since 1974. Never opened on-page.
- **The Red String** — one length of string, donated anonymously to the Lost & Found Authority in 2015, still awaiting recognition.
- **The Wooden Spoon (Artifact #442)** — Museum exhibit, estimated age: Tuesday.
- **The Emotional Support Stapler** — believed to be one of the staplers from the Great Stapler Migration; now bolted to a desk in the Committee's records room.
- **The Napkin Treaty** — nine pages, concerns one goat, displayed at the Museum beside a "context pending" placard.

## Calendar of holidays (for future seasonal content — see `season` field)

- **Founding Day** (observed the day of the Committee Vote of 1987) — every institution issues a routine memo; none acknowledge the others are doing the same thing.
- **Recognition Week** — the Lost & Found Authority's annual reminder that several items remain awaiting recognition; nothing is ever actually reunited during it.
- **The Tuesday Compromise Observance** — held on a Tuesday, naturally; commemorates nothing specific, which is the point.
- **Static Cling Remembrance Day** — the Bureau's quietest holiday.

## Famous / recurring personnel (referenceable, never given dialogue)

- **The Curator** (Museum) — never named, always "the Curator"; has served since before any current record can confirm.
- **A Concerned Neighbor** (Wildlife Reports) — the recurring, anonymous filer of most bulletins; presumed to be one person, treated as an institution unto themselves.
- **The Quorum** (Committee) — never a named individual; decisions are attributed to "the Committee" collectively, even when barely quorate.
