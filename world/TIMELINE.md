# Shared Timeline

> Internal reference. These are the recurring historical events Daily Items should call back to (via the `tags` and `callbacks` fields in `content.ts`) rather than inventing a new incident every time. Never fully explained in any single Daily Item — the reader assembles the picture across institutions and months. Slugs are the `tags` values to use in code.

| Slug | Year | Event | Institutions involved |
|---|---|---|---|
| `committee-vote-1987` | 1987 | **The Committee Vote of 1987.** A temporary committee, formed to resolve a seating-chart dispute, voted unanimously to make itself permanent "for the public good." Nobody present recalls what the public good in question was. This is the founding event of the Committee for Poor Decisions. | Committee |
| `stapler-migration` | 1988 | **The Great Stapler Migration.** Over six weeks, every stapler in the municipal building complex relocated, overnight, to a different department than the one it started in. No mechanism was ever identified. A task force was convened and later became the Committee's Subcommittee on Office Supplies, which still meets. | Committee, Lost & Found Authority |
| `filing-cabinet-1974` | 1974 | **The Filing Cabinet Reclassification of 1974.** A single filing cabinet containing "historically significant nonsense" was claimed by both the Museum and the Department. Resolved by declaring it a joint exhibit, which it remains. Neither institution has opened it since. | Museum, Department |
| `duck-harbor-master` | 1622 | **The Duck Harbor Master Precedent.** A Dutch village elected a duck as harbor master; the duck served two full terms without incident. Cited by the Committee ever since as legal precedent whenever a decision is questioned ("the duck served two terms; this is fine"). | Museum, Committee |
| `goose-incident` | 1996 | **The Goose Incident.** A goose entered a Committee budget meeting through an open window and, by remaining until the vote concluded, was recorded in the minutes as present and non-objecting. The Department later peer-reviewed the goose's decision-making process and found it "methodologically sound." Its descendants are tracked to this day. | Committee, Department, Wildlife |
| `tuesday-compromise` | 2003 | **The Tuesday Compromise.** After an unresolvable dispute over Museum operating hours, all sides agreed the matter would take effect "on Tuesday." It is still Tuesday, procedurally, for this purpose. Cited across institutions as a fallback resolution whenever consensus fails. | Museum, Committee, Notices |
| `wednesday-that-wasnt` | 2004 | **The Wednesday That Wasn't.** An attempt to extend the Tuesday Compromise's logic to Wednesday failed for reasons Official Public Notices declined to specify. Notices published only: "Wednesday remains Wednesday, for now." | Notices, Committee |
| `sock-inquiry` | 2011 | **The Missing Sock Inquiry.** The Lost & Found Authority's oldest continuously open case. The Department has published (as yet unconfirmed) research suggesting socks are not lost but "promoted." Wildlife Reports maintains a person of interest: one specific raccoon. Status: ongoing. | Lost & Found Authority, Department, Wildlife |
| `red-string-donation` | 2015 | **The Red String Donation.** One length of red string was submitted to the Lost & Found Authority with no accompanying explanation, note, or claimant. It remains "awaiting recognition." The Office of Public Clarifications was asked to clarify its origin and formally declined. | Lost & Found Authority, Office of Public Clarifications |
| `reorg-2009` | 2009 | **The Reorganization of 2009.** The Bureau of Mild Emergencies and the Office of Public Clarifications were both established this year, for reasons neither office will confirm or deny confirming. Widely assumed to be connected. Never proven. | Bureau, Office of Public Clarifications |
| `static-cling-anomaly` | 2018 | **The Static Cling Anomaly.** The Bureau's most-cited past response: a "situation of moderate note" involving persistent, unexplained static cling across three municipal buildings. De-escalated to baseline after four days. The Office of Public Clarifications received 200+ requests for clarification and issued zero. | Bureau, Office of Public Clarifications |
| `napkin-war` | 1801 | **The Treaty of Overly Specific Terms.** A minor kingdom went to war over the correct method of folding a napkin. The resulting nine-page treaty concerns only the ownership of one goat and is displayed at the Museum, unexplained, beside a placard reading "context pending." | Museum |
| `emotionally-haunted-ruling` | 1975 | **The Mildly Haunted Ruling.** A local court legally declared a man in Ohio "mildly haunted." The ruling was never appealed and is cited by the Bureau as the earliest precedent for classifying a situation as "concerning but not actionable." | Museum, Bureau |

## Notes for future entries

- Every event above should resurface at least once more across a different institution before a brand-new event is introduced. The world should feel finite and cross-referenced, not infinite and disposable.
- New events get added here first, with a slug, before they appear in any Daily Item's `tags`.
- Nothing on this list is ever resolved on-page. "Ongoing," "pending," and "unconfirmed" are permanent states, not placeholders.
- **Callbacks reward, never gate (Chip, Session 5).** A reader who has never seen `goose-incident` before should still find `deir-3` complete and funny on its own. Cross-references are a bonus for a second visit, not a comprehension requirement for the first. If an item only works when you already know the referenced event, it's failed the Museum Test regardless of how good the continuity is.
