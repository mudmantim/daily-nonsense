# Institution Profiles

> Internal reference. Each profile is the source of truth for that institution's voice. New Daily Items should be checked against their institution's profile before being added to `content.ts`.

---

## The Department of Extremely Important Research (`deir`)

**Purpose:** Rigorous, peer-reviewed research into matters of zero practical consequence.

**Personality:** Earnest, underfunded, allergic to speculation. Every conclusion is backed by methodology the Department considers impeccable. They are not being silly on purpose — silliness would imply awareness, and the Department has none.

**Motto:** "Probably." (Est. 1957)

**Voice:** Never "discovered" — always "concluded," "observed," "findings indicate." Every statement is hedged with the confidence of peer review, never the confidence of certainty. Funding is always "requested," never "needed."

**Recurring formats:** New Study Finds, Research Finding, Scientific Paper, Press Release, Safety Bulletin.

**Recurring themes:** Studies of mundane human and household behavior, treated with the apparatus of hard science. Sleep, soup, doorknobs, socks, geese.

**Refuses to discuss:** Why any of this is funded. Whether it has ever been funded.

**Connected events:** `filing-cabinet-1974` (with the Museum), `goose-incident` (with the Committee and Wildlife), `sock-inquiry` (with the Lost & Found Authority).

**Relationships:** Quiet rivalry with the Museum over the Jointly-Held Filing Cabinet. Occasionally peer-reviews the Committee's decisions after the fact, unasked.

## The Museum of Useless History (`museum`)

**Purpose:** Preservation and exhibition of history nobody requested preserved.

**Personality:** Nothing is fake to the Museum. Everything matters. It just shouldn't. The Curator (never named) loves every exhibit without irony.

**Motto:** "This definitely happened. Somewhere. Probably."

**Voice:** Never "old" — always "historically underappreciated." Uncertain exhibits get a placard reading "context pending" rather than an explanation.

**Recurring formats:** Historical Discovery, Today in History, Newly Unearthed Artifact, Curator's Notes.

**Recurring themes:** Minor historical disputes escalated to treaties and standoffs; objects assigned reverence disproportionate to their importance.

**Refuses to discuss:** The actual contents of the Jointly-Held Filing Cabinet.

**Connected events:** `filing-cabinet-1974`, `duck-harbor-master`, `tuesday-compromise`, `napkin-war`, `emotionally-haunted-ruling`.

**Relationships:** Co-owns the Filing Cabinet with the Department. Cites the Duck Harbor Master Precedent to the Committee more often than the Committee would like.

## The Committee for Poor Decisions (`committee`)

**Purpose:** Governs by consensus, irrespective of the quality of the consensus reached.

**Personality:** Not idiots — optimists. Every decision sounded reasonable in the room at the time. The Committee never disbands, per its own founding precedent.

**Motto:** "We do not recommend this. We insist on it."

**Voice:** Never "mistake" — always "approved outcome" or "decision of record." Attendance is reported collectively ("the Committee"), never as named individuals.

**Recurring formats:** Official Recommendation, Meeting Minutes, Approved Motion, Emergency Decision.

**Recurring themes:** Motions passed unanimously that should not have been. Consensus as a substitute for correctness.

**Refuses to discuss:** What its original 1987 mandate actually was.

**Connected events:** `committee-vote-1987` (founding), `stapler-migration`, `goose-incident`, `duck-harbor-master`, `tuesday-compromise`, `wednesday-that-wasnt`.

**Relationships:** Frequently cites the Museum's precedents. The Department has peer-reviewed at least one of its votes without being asked.

## Neighborhood Wildlife Reports (`wildlife`)

**Purpose:** Documentation of local animal activity with the procedural weight of an intelligence bulletin.

**Personality:** The wildlife isn't magical — the reporting is. Filed with the seriousness normally reserved for matters of national security. Attributed to "a concerned neighbor," presumed to be one person functioning as an institution.

**Motto:** "Filed by a concerned neighbor who is definitely not overreacting."

**Voice:** Never "attacked" or "aggressive" — always "engaged directly" or "displayed confidence." Never "guess" — "assessed with reasonable certainty."

**Recurring formats:** Animal Interview, Incident Report, Citizen Complaint, Wildlife Alert.

**Recurring themes:** Squirrels, geese, crows, and raccoons engaged in behavior treated as calculated and possibly coordinated.

**Refuses to discuss:** The identity of the concerned neighbor.

**Connected events:** `goose-incident`, `sock-inquiry` (a specific raccoon is a person of interest).

**Relationships:** Supplied testimony to the Department's goose peer review. Maintains an open suspect in the Lost & Found Authority's oldest case.

## The Lost & Found Authority (`lostfound`)

**Purpose:** Custodianship of items separated from their owners, for however long that takes.

**Personality:** Secretly the most emotional institution in the apparatus, though it would never phrase it that way. Every object is treated as though it is still waiting, patiently, and always will be.

**Motto:** "Please claim within a reasonable, undefined amount of time."

**Voice:** Never "lost" or "unclaimed" — always "awaiting recognition" or "patiently held."

**Recurring formats:** Item Recovered, Claim Status, Custodial Note.

**Recurring themes:** Ordinary objects described with quiet, unearned dignity. Cases that never close.

**Refuses to discuss:** Whether it believes anything will actually be reclaimed.

**Connected events:** `stapler-migration`, `sock-inquiry` (its oldest open case), `red-string-donation`.

**Relationships:** Received unexplained overflow from the Stapler Migration. Asked the Office of Public Clarifications to clarify the Red String's origin; was refused.

## Official Public Notices (`notices`)

**Purpose:** Informing the public of matters the public did not request information on, under an authority that may or may not exist.

**Personality:** Never emotional, never sarcastic. Quietly informs. Assumes full legitimacy without ever establishing it.

**Motto:** "Issued by an authority that may or may not exist."

**Voice:** Never "don't" or "stop" — always "residents are reminded."

**Recurring formats:** Public Notice, Reminder, Distribution Memo.

**Recurring themes:** Reminders about matters no one raised. Time and scheduling disputes resolved by decree.

**Refuses to discuss:** Which authority, specifically, issues these notices.

**Connected events:** `tuesday-compromise`, `wednesday-that-wasnt`.

**Relationships:** The closest thing the apparatus has to a public voice; frequently used by other institutions to announce compromises they don't want attributed to themselves.

## The Bureau of Mild Emergencies (`bureau`)

**Purpose:** Response and classification for situations that are technically emergencies and technically fine.

**Personality:** Calm to the point of unnerving. Nothing is ever an emergency; everything is a "situation of moderate note." De-escalation is the only outcome the Bureau recognizes.

**Motto:** "Concerning, but not actionable."

**Voice:** Never "emergency," "crisis," or "resolved" — always "situation of moderate note" and "de-escalated to baseline."

**Recurring formats:** Situation Report, Advisory (Non-Urgent), De-escalation Notice.

**Recurring themes:** Minor, ambiguous incidents handled with full procedural gravity and zero visible urgency.

**Refuses to discuss:** Why it was founded the same year as the Office of Public Clarifications.

**Connected events:** `reorg-2009` (founding), `static-cling-anomaly`, `emotionally-haunted-ruling` (cites as precedent).

**Relationships:** Founded alongside the Office of Public Clarifications under circumstances neither will confirm. Their situation reports are frequently the subject of clarification requests that go nowhere.

## The Office of Public Clarifications (`clarifications`)

**Purpose:** To clarify matters of public confusion. Has not yet done so.

**Personality:** Polite, procedurally responsive, substantively empty. Every inquiry receives a reply. No reply contains an answer.

**Motto:** "Further clarification is not available at this time."

**Voice:** Every statement functions as a non-clarification, phrased as though it were helpful.

**Recurring formats:** Clarification Request (Response), Statement of Non-Clarification, Public Inquiry Log.

**Recurring themes:** Bureaucratic non-answers to reasonable questions, delivered with total sincerity and no acknowledgment of the irony.

**Refuses to discuss:** Everything, formally and on the record.

**Connected events:** `reorg-2009` (founding), `red-string-donation` (declined to clarify), `static-cling-anomaly` (received the most requests of its history, clarified none).

**Relationships:** Founded alongside the Bureau of Mild Emergencies. On record having declined to clarify matters for the Lost & Found Authority at least once.
