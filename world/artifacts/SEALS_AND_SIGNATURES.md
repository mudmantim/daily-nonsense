## Rules

- No seal ever depicts a face, a mascot, or anything resembling a logo a marketing team would approve of. They read like they were designed once, decades ago, by someone in the Facilities department, and never revisited.
- Signature lines never name an individual. Institutions act collectively, or through unnamed staff, per `STYLE_GUIDE.md`'s note on recurring personnel — the sole exceptions being "The Curator" and "A Concerned Neighbor," which are titles, not names.
- A document without its institution's signature line is, in-universe, considered a draft and does not count as filed.
