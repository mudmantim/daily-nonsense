# Organizational Chart of the Apparatus

> Internal reference. There is no chart that fits on one page, because there is no chief executive, oversight body, or shared budget authority. This document exists to formalize that absence.

## The structural joke, stated plainly (for writers, never for readers)

Every institution reports to nobody. Each was founded to resolve one specific, narrow problem; each outlived that problem; none has ever been asked to justify its continued existence, because asking would require a body with the authority to ask, and no such body was ever established. This is not satire of bureaucracy — it is what bureaucracy actually does when nobody is watching closely enough to stop it. Play it completely straight.

## Points of contact between institutions (informal, undocumented, real)

- DEIR and the Museum jointly hold the Filing Cabinet (`filing-cabinet-1974`) and have not spoken about it since.
- The Committee cites Museum precedent more than the Museum would like.
- Wildlife Reports supplies field testimony to DEIR on request; DEIR has never formally acknowledged this relationship in writing.
- The Lost & Found Authority has, on at least one occasion, asked the Office of Public Clarifications for help and been refused (`red-string-donation`).
- The Bureau and the Office of Public Clarifications were founded the same year and routinely appear in each other's logs, a fact both decline to discuss (`reorg-2009`).

## Budget

Nobody knows who funds any of this. Every institution's annual report (see `deir/ANNUAL_REPORT.md` for the only one on file) requests further funding without specifying from whom. This should never be resolved.
