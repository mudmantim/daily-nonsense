# Department of Extremely Important Research — Employee Handbook (Excerpt)

> In-universe document. Internal reference for future Daily Items and copy — establishes DEIR's institutional texture so any writer can produce new material in its voice. Ref: `DEIR-HB-0001`.

## Welcome to the Department

You have joined an institution founded in 1957 on the principle that no subject is too small to deserve rigorous, peer-reviewed attention. We ask that you carry that principle into every finding you help conclude.

## Section 1: Conduct

1.1. Staff are never "excited" about a finding. Staff are "satisfied that the methodology held."

1.2. Under no circumstances should a researcher describe a result as "surprising." All results are, by definition, consistent with what sufficiently rigorous research would conclude. If a result feels surprising, the study was insufficiently rigorous, and must be redone.

1.3. Lab coats are optional but strongly encouraged, including for research that does not involve a laboratory.

## Section 2: The Peer Review Process

2.1. Every finding must be reviewed by at least one other staff member before publication.

2.2. Peer reviewers may not decline to review a finding on the grounds that the finding is self-evidently unimportant. Importance is not a criterion this Department evaluates.

2.3. If a peer reviewer is unavailable for comment, the finding may proceed to publication with a note that reviewers were unavailable for comment. This is not considered a procedural failure. It happens often, and is itself, at this point, a documented pattern.

## Section 3: Language

3.1. We do not "discover" things. We "conclude," "observe," or note that "findings indicate." A discovery implies we did not already suspect it. We always already suspected it.

3.2. Funding is always "requested," never "needed." Need implies a deficiency in prior planning. Requests imply optimism.

## Section 4: On the Filing Cabinet

4.1. New staff will, within their first month, be shown the Jointly-Held Filing Cabinet (see `filing-cabinet-1974`; joint custody with the Museum of Useless History). It has not been opened since 1974. It will not be opened during your tenure. Do not ask why. Nobody currently employed knows why, and asking only prolongs the meeting in which you ask.

## Section 5: On the Goose

5.1. Staff hired after 1996 may hear reference to "the goose" (see `goose-incident`). This refers to a specific, historical goose, whose decision-making process this Department formally peer-reviewed and found methodologically sound. It is not a metaphor. There was, at the time, an actual goose.

## Closing note

You will, at some point, be asked by a friend or relative what exactly the Department researches. The correct answer is: extremely important matters, the importance of which is presently under continued review.
