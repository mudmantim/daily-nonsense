# DEIR Log of Rejected Research Proposals (Selected Entries)

> In-universe document. Ref: `DEIR-RP-LOG`. A future Daily Item format ("Rejected Proposal") could draw directly from entries like these.

**Proposal 0441:** *A Longitudinal Study of Whether the Last Slice of Pizza Gains Emotional Weight Over Time.* Rejected — not for lack of merit, but because a prior, unrelated Department study already established this finding informally in 2019 and was never peer-reviewed. Resubmission encouraged pending retrieval of the original notes, which are believed to be in the Filing Cabinet.

**Proposal 0502:** *A Comparative Analysis of Doorknob Sentience Across Three Municipal Buildings.* Rejected — sample size (12,000 doorknobs) was deemed sufficient, but the proposal's control group (doorknobs with no known opinion) could not be reliably distinguished from the test group.

**Proposal 0517:** *Determining Whether Geese Possess Institutional Memory.* Rejected on procedural grounds — this question was already closed by the 1996 peer review of goose decision-making (`goose-incident`), and reopening it would require the goose's participation, which could not be secured.

**Proposal 0563:** *A Study Proposing to Definitively Resolve the Sock Migration Question.* Rejected. The Department's position, per `DEIR-SB-####`, is that ongoing uncertainty is itself a finding, and resolving it would require closing an active line of research the Department is not prepared to close.

**Proposal 0601:** *A Request to Finally Open the Filing Cabinet.* Rejected unanimously, and unusually quickly, by a peer review panel that convened for four minutes.
