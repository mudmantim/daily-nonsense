# Bureau of Mild Emergencies — Situation Classification Manual (Excerpt)

> In-universe document. Ref: `BME-PM-0001`.

## Classification Scale

The Bureau recognizes exactly one operational category: **Situation of Moderate Note.** There is no higher category. Escalation beyond "moderate note" is, per founding charter, the jurisdiction of an agency the Bureau has never been able to identify.

## Intake Procedure

1. A situation is reported.
2. A Situation Report is opened under reference `BME-SR-####` per `FILING_SYSTEM.md`. This number never changes, regardless of how the situation later resolves or is reclassified in format (Advisory, De-escalation Notice).
3. The situation is assessed. All assessments conclude the situation is concerning, but not actionable.
4. The situation is monitored until it de-escalates to baseline on its own, which it always does, eventually.
5. A De-escalation Notice is filed. This is the only closing action the Bureau takes on any situation.

## On the Office of Public Clarifications

The Bureau's Situation Reports routinely generate requests for clarification, which are forwarded to the Office of Public Clarifications (est. the same year as the Bureau, 2009, see `reorg-2009`). The Bureau does not track how many of these requests are answered, because, per its own records, none have been.

## Precedents Cited

The Mildly Haunted Ruling of 1975 (`emotionally-haunted-ruling`) remains the Bureau's earliest and most-cited precedent for the "concerning, but not actionable" standard.
